Name:The Goliath
Classification:Multi Host Construct

───── PHYSICAL RE-CONSTRUCTION ─────

Mass Composition:A fused amalgam of 10-15 human hosts. The skeletal structures are braided together into a singular, reinforced chassis. Stands approximately 9-11 feet in height.
The Neural Core:A secondary nervous system visible along the spine, consisting of multiple brain stems linked in parallel. This allows the unit to process combat data and environmental variables with machine-like efficiency.
Hydraulic Muscle Tissue:Muscles are hyper-oxygenated and pressurized. The unit utilizes hydraulic-like pressure to deliver strikes capable of crushing light armored vehicles and breaching reinforced bunkers.

[APEX CAPABILITIES]

TACTICAL CAPABILITIES
  • Bloom Integration: The Brute can "plug" into the arterial brambles of a Stage 2 environment to rapidly repair cellular damage or update its tactical heuristics.
  • Seismic Roar: Emits a localized, high-decibel shockwave that shatters glass and disorients the equilibrium of un-infected personnel within a 50ft radius.
  • Reclamation Hooks: Calcified bone-chitin protrusions on the forearms used to "harvest" living targets. It prioritizes capturing targets alive to drag them back to the Reclamation Vats.

───── BEHAVIORAL PROFILE ─────

The Protector Instinct:The Brute serves as a primary guardian. It typically remains dormant within a Bloom until the central network detects a significant threat or a breach of the Neural Well.
Strategic Suppression:Exhibits high-level problem solving. It will actively use the mass of neutralized Stage 1 entities as physical shields or projectiles to dismantle entrenched fire-teams.
FIELD ADVISORY:

Engagement with a Stage 3 Brute is a critical hazard for standard infantry. If visual contact is established, evacuate the immediate zone and authorize heavy munitions. Do not allow the Brute to reach the perimeter; it is capable of tearing through blast doors in under 60 seconds.
